ROSE HACK Team Organizers FAQ About Us
Rose Hack
January 15-16, 2022
Virtual Hackathon @ UC Riverside
Rose Hack is UC Riverside’s women-centric hackathon, founded by the female leaders of the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM-W) and the Society of Women Engineers (SWE). Rose Hack’s mission is to encourage individuals from underrepresented groups in STEM to create a strong network together and build an amazing project through their shared passions in Science and Technology. Come for a lively weekend of innovation, face masks, discovering and creating new technology, networking, mentorship, and much more! Keep in mind that all participants are expected to abide by the MLH Code of Conduct.

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a tech-focused event where individuals come together to build projects over the weekend. Projects can be an app, website, or anything that you want to build!

How do I apply for Rose Hack?

Applications are open! Registration form is linked above.

Who can attend?

Any high school or undergraduate student may attend virtually! In order to ensure the safety of our organizers and in-person participants, only UCR students will be eligible to attend in-person this year. Priority will be given to women or other underrepresented groups in tech.

How will a virtual Rose Hack work?

We have online platforms to host Rose Hack and will send more detailed information/invites to your email once you are accepted.

Will there be swag?

Swag will be provided to all participants in the United States! For international participants, unfortunately, we will be unable to ship swag outside of the United States; however, if you win a prize, we will ensure you get it no matter where in the world you are participating from!

What safety precautions is Rose Hack taking?

Opening & closing ceremonies, mentors, and judging will be held virtually.

What should I bring?

Make sure to have ready your laptop, chargers, and anything else you need for 24 hours of hacking.

I am not female. Should I still apply?

Absolutely! All high school and undergraduate students from any background and ethnicities are welcome to apply!

What if I don't know how to code?

Rose Hack is open to all skill levels! We will also host workshops and provide awesome mentors that can help you learnand build your amazing project!

Do I have to be on a team?

Though it is not required, we highly recommend that you form or join a team. Teams can have up to 4 people. We will have a team formation session if you cannot find a team before then.

How much do I have to pay?

Rose Hack is completely free. Enjoy a weekend filled with activities, learning, and maybe even snacks & swag!

When should I show up/leave?

Participants should plan to start hacking early Saturday (1/15) morning and finish by Sunday (1/16) afternoon. A detailed schedule will be relased soon!

Can I start hacking before the event?

All projects for Rose Hack must be made during the weekend. If you start hacking before the event, your project will be disqualified. However, planning and gathering resources before the hackathon is allowed.

Are there travel reimbursements?

Unfortunately, we won't be able to provide travel reimbursements this year.

Where is Rose Hack hosted?

Rose Hack is hosted in sunny California at the University of California, Riverside.
